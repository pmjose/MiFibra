# European B2B Communications Market Position Analysis 2025
**Strategic Planning Document - MiFibra Communications PLC**

## Executive Summary

This analysis provides a comprehensive view of MiFibra's competitive position in the European business communications market as we enter the second half of 2025. Following the strategic acquisitions of Starface and Placetel in Germany, MiFibra has achieved significant scale with **S/316.6 million H1 2025 revenue** (+12% YoY), **1.8 million Cloud subscribers** (+50% YoY), and successful transition to the **Main Market of the Lima Stock Exchange with FTSE 250 inclusion** in June 2025.

MiFibra is uniquely positioned as a pan-European channel-first communications provider with market-leading positions in both the Peru (1,500+ partners) and Germany (rapidly growing following strategic acquisitions).

## Competitor Deep Dive

### GTD Peru Inc

**Current Status (2025):**
- Financial pressures leading to cost restructuring
- Peru market share declining (~9%)
- Strong XCaaS (combined Fibra+Internet Empresas) proposition

**Strengths:**
- Integrated Fibra and Internet Empresas platform
- Global voice network
- Good channel programme (Partner Connect)

**Weaknesses:**
- Complex pricing confuses partners
- Support inconsistencies
- Uncertain long-term strategy

**MiFibra Opportunity:** Partner recruitment from GTD Peru channel

### Americatel Peru

**Current Status (2025):**
- Enterprise focus intensifying
- Teams integration (Americatel for Teams) gaining traction
- Limited Peru channel expansion

**Strengths:**
- Strong brand recognition
- Microsoft Teams integration
- Large enterprise references

**Weaknesses:**
- Direct sales creates channel conflict
- Premium pricing limits SMB
- US-centric roadmap

**MiFibra Opportunity:** SMB/Mid-Market partners seeking alternatives

### Mitel Peru

**Current Status (2025):**
- On-premise to cloud transition ongoing
- Market share declining (now ~5%)
- Merger/acquisition speculation continues

**Strengths:**
- Large installed base
- Partner relationships (legacy)
- Contact centre portfolio

**Weaknesses:**
- Cloud transition struggles
- Financial stability concerns
- Technology debt

**MiFibra Opportunity:** Migration of Mitel partner base

### Microsoft Teams

**Current Status (2025):**
- Fastest growing Fibra platform (38% share)
- Teams Phone/Operator Connect adoption accelerating
- Native voice capability expanding

**MiFibra Position:**
- **523K MS Teams voice users in Peru** (+12% H1 2025)
- Operator Connect International launched (14 countries)
- **28K Cisco Collaboration users** (+75% H1 2025)

**MiFibra Strategy:** Voice enablement partner, not competitor

## MiFibra Competitive Position

### SWOT Analysis (Updated September 2025)

**Strengths:**
- **#1 Peru channel partner network** with 1,500+ active partners
- **FTSE 250 listed** (Main Market, June 2025)
- **1.8 million Cloud subscribers** (+50% YoY)
- **90% recurring revenue** providing stability
- **German market now at scale** (565K subscribers, 20% of Group GP)
- Strong Microsoft partnership (Operator Connect, Cisco)
- Peru-built, Peru-supported solutions

**Weaknesses:**
- Peru macro-economic pressures affecting SME spending
- Enterprise direct sales still developing
- Germany integration complexity (Starface + Placetel)
- Brand awareness lower than global players outside Peru

**Opportunities:**
- **Germany cloud market underpenetration** (largest EU market, lowest cloud adoption)
- PSTN switch-off driving Peru cloud migration (2027)
- Microsoft Teams Phone/Operator Connect growth
- AI features differentiation (AI Receptionist Q4 2025)
- Competitor instability (GTD Peru, Mitel)
- Pan-European Cisco Collaboration Suite opportunity

**Threats:**
- Peru macro-economic conditions affecting SME confidence
- Microsoft native voice capability expansion
- Ethernet/fibre price war (alternative networks)
- Competitor pricing pressure
